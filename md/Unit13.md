### Scene I: Stay Calm, Please

*A guest asks the front desk clerk about earthquakes in Taiwan.*  
**G: Guest | F: Front Desk Clerk**

**G:** Excuse me. It says here in my **guidebook** that there are many **earthquakes** in Hualien. Is that true?

**F:** I'm afraid it is.

**G:** What should I do if one **strikes** the hotel?

**F:** Well, most of them are so small that you can't feel them.

**G:** What if it's a big one?

**F:** In that case, please **stay calm**. It's important that you don't **panic**.

**G:** OK, I'll try to remember that.

**F:** You should also **take cover** somewhere safe, like in a doorway or corner. Then stay there until the **ground** stops shaking.

**G:** Is there anything else that I should know?

**F:** Keep clear of windows or tall furniture that may fall over. And remember not to use the elevator.

**G:** I won't. I guess I'll just hide under my desk in my room.

**F:** Yes, that's very **wise**. You could get **injured** by falling trees or building parts if you go outside.

**G:** It sounds really **scary**!

**F:** Well, we try as hard as we can to protect the safety of every guest. I'm sure you'll be alright.

### Scene II & III: May I Have Your Attention?

#### SCENE II — Fire Alarm Announcement

*A front desk clerk makes an emergency announcement.*

> Attention guests. The fire alarm has been **activated**. Please do not panic. Leave your luggage in your rooms and walk slowly to the stairs at each end of the hall. Do not take the elevators. Move to the lobby in an **orderly** way. Remember, do not push or **shove** the other guests. The hotel staff will meet you in the lobby and then direct you outside.

> Attention employees. Please assist our guests in **evacuating** the hotel. Please check the floor you are on to make sure all of our guests are out of the rooms. Then, **proceed** to the stairs and give assistance as needed. When all of the guests are out of the hotel, please meet in the lobby.

#### SCENE III — Fire Drill Announcement

*The hotel holds a fire **drill**.*

> Can I have your attention? To **promote** safety **standards**, we will be holding a fire drill. I repeat: this is a drill, and not for real. Our staff members will assist you in proceeding to the nearest fire exit. Please remain there until the fire alarm **shuts off**. It is **of the utmost importance** that you do not go into the stairwell or use the elevators. We apologize for any inconvenience this causes. Thank you for your **cooperation**.

### Scene IV: Maybe You Should See a Doctor

#### SCENE IV (a) — Guest Feels Ill

*A guest doesn't feel well.*  
**F: Front Desk Clerk | G: Guest**

**F:** Front Desk. May I help you?

**G:** This is Mrs. Johnson in Room 705. My son is coming down with a cold. Do you have any cold medicine?

**F:** I'm afraid we can't **dispense** medicine, Mrs. Johnson. It's against the law.

**G:** But my son is really sick. It looks like he has the **chills**.

**G:** Could you phone her?

**F:** We do have a doctor on call.

**G:** How long will it take for the doctor to arrive at the hotel?

**F:** She can get here within an hour.

**G:** Certainly. I hope it's not too **serious**, Mrs. Johnson.

**G:** Thanks. I guess we'll just have to wait for the time being.

**F:** Don't worry. The doctor will be here **shortly**.

#### SCENE IV (b) — Guest Has an Injury

*A guest falls off her bike and hurt herself.*  
**F: Front Desk Clerk | G: Guest**

**F:** What happened to you, Ms. Ritter?

**G:** I fell off the bike and **scraped** my **knee**.

**F:** *(Taking out the first aid kit)* We'll get you cleaned up right now.

**G:** Ouch! That **stings**!

**F:** I'm just cleaning the **wound**. You don't want it to get **infected**, do you?

**G:** No, I don't. Do you have a bandage in there?

**F:** Yes, there's one right here. Let's get your knee bandaged up and then you can take a rest.

**G:** But I'm not finished with my bike ride.

**F:** Are you sure, Ms. Ritter? Your knee looks pretty bad.

**G:** I think I'll be OK.
