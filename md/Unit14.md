### Scene I: Coming in for an Interview

*Jennifer applies for a position as a front desk clerk.*  
**J: Job Applicant | R: Receptionist | M: Ms. Thompson**

**J:** Excuse me, sir. I'm Jennifer Yang. I have an **appointment** with Ms. Thompson at 10:00 a.m. sharp.

**R:** Yes. Ms. Thompson is waiting for you. I'll take you to her office.

**J:** Thank you.

*(The receptionist **knocks** on the door of Ms. Thompson's office.)*

**R:** Come on in, please.

**R:** Ms. Thompson, Ms. Yang is here to apply for the front desk clerk **position**.

**M:** Yes, I'm expecting her. Thank you.

*(The receptionist leaves.)*

**M:** Ms. Yang, welcome. I'm in charge of interviewing applicants for the job.

**J:** Yes, Ms. Thompson. Thank you for calling me in for an interview.

**M:** Well, we're always looking for **qualified** new staff. Please have a seat.

**J:** Thank you.

**M:** Well, Ms. Yang, what kind of work experience do you have?

**J:** I did a year-long internship at the ABC Hotel. I worked as a housekeeper and a front desk clerk.

**M:** What exactly were your **duties**?

**J:** Well, I cleaned and stocked guest rooms. I also took phone requests from guests, especially foreign guests.

**M:** I see. And could you describe yourself a little?

**J:** I'm very **hardworking**. I have a good memory for names and faces. Besides, I'm **outgoing** and a team player.

### Scene II: When Can You Start?

*Jennifer answers Ms. Thompson's questions at a job interview.*  
**M: Ms. Thompson | J: Jennifer**

**M:** Do you speak Taiwanese?

**J:** Yes, I speak it **fluently**. I can also speak English. My TOEIC score was 830. Plus, I'm able to **communicate** in Japanese.

**M:** Very well. What about your computer skills?

**J:** Well, I'm familiar with programs, like Word, PowerPoint, Excel, and other industry-related software.

**M:** Good. Do you have any questions?

**J:** What are the working hours?

**M:** From three p.m. to midnight. But **once in a while**, you'll have to work the graveyard shift.

**J:** I understand. And what kind of salary do you usually offer someone with my qualifications?

**M:** We give a **monthly** salary of NT$28,000 to NT$33,000.

**J:** Alright. Do you provide any **medical** or meal plans?

**M:** All full-time employees are offered health **insurance** and two free meals per day.

**J:** I see.

**M:** One last question, Ms. Yang. If we hired you, what **contributions** could you make to our hotel?

**J:** Well, I believe I can make a good first **impression**. I have strong **interpersonal** skills. And I always get along well with both guests and coworkers.

**M:** That's very important. When can you start?

**J:** Anytime after June 25th.

**M:** Well, then. We'll let you know of our decision by the end of the week.

**J:** Alright. Thank you.

**M:** You're welcome. Good-bye.

### Language Center — Scene II: Sentence Patterns

**Conditional Sentences (Unreal Present):**

- If I were you, I would be more polite to the manager.
- If I were given the chance, I'm sure I'd prove to be a good employee.
- If the guest complained, I would try to solve the problem.
- If you wore a little makeup, you might look more professional.
- If the weather improved, Doug could go for a hike.

> **Note:** When the supposition is contrary to the present facts, *be* takes the form of *were* for all persons.

### Scene III: Writing a Winning Cover Letter

*Track 69*

#### Sample Cover Letter

**Jennifer Yang**  
231 Jiuru 1st Rd., Sanmin District  
Kaohsiung, Taiwan  

December 21st

**Selma Thompson**  
Front Desk Manager  
Sparkling Hotel  
2994 Zhongshan N. Rd., Sec. 4  
Taipei, Taiwan  

Dear Ms. Thompson,

I am writing in regard to the front desk clerk position advertised on your hotel's Web site.

For your review, I have enclosed a copy of my résumé. As you can see, I will graduate from Kaohsiung Hospitality College this June with a BA in hotel management. Last year, I successfully completed a year-long internship at the ABC Hotel. I believe I would fit in well with your team as I am outgoing, friendly, and have good language skills.

I am available for an interview at your convenience. I can be reached at (07) 345-8295. I look forward to meeting you and will make the most of any opportunity you give me. Thank you for your consideration. I hope to hear from you soon.

Sincerely,  
Jennifer Yang  
*Jennifer Yang*

*Enclosed: résumé*

---

**How to Start a Cover Letter:**

- I am writing in regard to…
- I am writing to answer…
- I am writing to respond to…
